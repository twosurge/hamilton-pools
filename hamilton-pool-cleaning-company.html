    How often should the pool guy come?

    Frequency depends on your pool size, usage, and skill level. For basic maintenance, every 1-2 weeks might suffice. If you prefer less hands-on work, weekly visits from a professional can keep your pool sparkling clean. Consider bi-weekly cleanings during peak season!

    What is the cheapest way to shock a pool?

    Liquid chlorine is often the most cost-effective way to shock a pool, especially for smaller volumes. Consider granular options if you deal with larger pools or require longer-lasting shock action. Consult a pool store for specific recommendations!

    What does 25 mean in swimming?

    In swimming, '25' typically refers to the distance of one lap in a standard 25-meter pool. It can also signify the length of a particular drill or set within a workout.

    How do you maintain a pool for beginners?

    Start simple! Regularly skim debris, brush surfaces every few days, and vacuum once or twice a week. Test your water chemistry weekly and adjust pH and chlorine levels as needed. Learn about basic shock treatments for occasional algae control. Don't hesitate to ask for help if anything feels overwhelming!

    What does KK mean in swimming?

    KK typically stands for 'kick only', meaning you're focusing on legwork without using your arms. This is often done with a kickboard or pull buoy to isolate your leg muscles.

    What happens if you put too much clarifier in your pool?

    Clarifier overdose can cloud your pool instead of clearing it! Excess clarifier clumps particles together, forming a hazy film instead of settling to the bottom for easy vacuuming. Follow recommended dosages and avoid exceeding them!

    How do you get dirt off the bottom of a pool without a vacuum?

    Manual methods work surprisingly well! Skim debris before tackling the bottom. Use a pool brush to loosen dirt, then sweep it towards the deep end using a pool broom. Finally, manually scoop or net the accumulated dirt from the deep end. It's a workout, but effective!

    What naturally kills algae in a good way?

    Sunlight and proper water balance are natural allies against algae. Maintain pH and other chemical levels within ideal ranges to hinder algae growth. Utilize shade management and skim debris regularly to further prevent their development!
