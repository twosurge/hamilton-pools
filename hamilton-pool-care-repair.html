    How often should a pool be cleaned?

    Frequency varies! Daily skimming and brushing are crucial. Vacuuming 2-3 times a week and weekly water testing are recommended. Shock once a week during peak season, adjusting based on specific needs and water quality!

    How do professionals clean pools?

    We follow a systematic approach! We test, adjust, and balance water chemistry first. Then, we skim debris, brush all surfaces, and vacuum the entire pool floor. We inspect equipment and adjust settings as needed. Finally, we test and rebalance the water to ensure optimal health!

    How do you clean a pool that hasn't been cleaned in years?

    Patience is key! Start with safety - secure any electrical components and test for potential hazards. Remove large debris and overgrown vegetation. Shock heavily to combat accumulated algae and bacteria. Scrub surfaces thoroughly and vacuum repeatedly to remove layers of sediment. Monitor and adjust water chemistry closely. Invest in professional help if needed for deep cleaning or equipment repairs!

    How many bags of shock do I need to open my pool?

    Shock dosage depends on your pool's volume and shock type. Consult the product instructions or reach out to a pool store for guidance. Don't guesstimate - an accurate measurement ensures effective algae control without overdoing it!

    Can I use vinegar instead of muriatic acid in pool?

    While vinegar has acidic properties, it's not strong enough for effective pool pH adjustment. Using vinegar might require larger quantities, making it impractical and potentially leaving a strong odor. Stick to pool-specific pH adjusters.
