    Do you shock a clean pool?

    Shocking isn't just for dirty pools! It's a preventive measure to eliminate built-up chlorine byproducts and kill any lingering algae or bacteria. Shock your pool every 1-2 weeks during peak season, even if it looks clean, to maintain its health and clarity!

    How often should you shock your pool?

    Frequency depends on factors like pool usage, weather, and chlorine levels. Generally, shock every 1-2 weeks during peak season and after heavy rains or bather influx. Test your water regularly and adjust shocking schedule as needed!

    What does baking soda do for a pool?

    Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) acts as a natural pH buffer, raising slightly acidic pool water to a more balanced range. This improves chlorine effectiveness, reduces eye irritation, and prevents corrosion. Use it sparingly alongside regular chemical balancing!

    What is the best for cleaning the pool?

    There's no single "best" way, it depends on your pool type and needs. A pool net and brush tackle everyday debris, while a vacuum tackles settled dirt. Chemical balancing keeps the water clean and healthy. Robotic cleaners offer hands-off cleaning, but may not reach every corner.
