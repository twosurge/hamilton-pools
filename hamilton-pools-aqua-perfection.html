    Can you put chlorine in a dirty pool?

    Chlorine alone won't solve a heavily dirty pool. First, remove large debris with a net. Skim the surface, then brush and sweep the bottom to loosen dirt. Vacuum to remove the loosened sediment. Only then add shock treatment followed by regular chlorine to maintain sanitation!

    Can you put too much baking soda in pool?

    Yes, you can! Adding too much baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) raises pH significantly, disrupting water balance and causing cloudy water or skin irritation. Stick to recommended dosages for pH adjustments and avoid overdoing it!

    Are in ground pool cleaners worth it?

    In-ground cleaners offer convenience and save time, but consider your needs and budget. Robotic cleaners handle routine cleaning, but might miss corners or require occasional manual assistance. Weigh factors like pool size, budget, and cleaning preferences before investing!

    What does P stand for in swimming?

    P has several potential meanings in swimming. It could be 'pull', where you swim with a pull buoy focusing on arm strength, or 'personal best', your fastest recorded time for a specific distance. Less commonly, it might represent 'pyramid', a training set with increasing or decreasing distances.

    What does BB mean in breaststroke?

    In swimming, "BB" or "BBT" stands for "breaststroke kick with body turns," a drill emphasizing leg work and body rotation during breaststroke turns.     What happens if you put too much muriatic acid in your pool?

    Adding too much acid can drastically lower pH, causing eye irritation, skin burns, and damage to pool equipment. It's crucial to test and adjust pH gradually. If you over-acidify, neutralize with baking soda or pool-specific pH increaser.

    How do I keep algae from coming back in my pool?

    Prevention is key! Maintain proper water chemistry, including regular testing and balancing. Skim debris daily, brush surfaces weekly, and vacuum frequently. Invest in a quality pool cover to minimize exposure to contaminants and sunlight that promote algae growth!

    In what order should I add chemicals to my pool?

    Timing matters! Generally, start with pH adjustments like acid or soda ash. Wait 30 minutes, then add chlorine or shock. Let settle for another 30 minutes before adding algaecides or clarifiers. This avoids unwanted chemical interactions!
