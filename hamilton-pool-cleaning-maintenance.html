    How long can water sit in a pool without chlorine?

    Don't risk it! Even stagnant water for a short period can breed algae and bacteria, making the pool unusable and potentially hazardous. Always maintain proper chlorine levels or utilize alternative sanitation methods to keep your pool safe and swimmable!

    Do you run the pump when shocking pool?

    Absolutely! Run the pump for at least 24 hours after shocking to circulate the chlorine throughout the pool and maximize its effectiveness. Proper circulation ensures all areas are treated and the shock doesn't settle and damage surfaces!

    Will baking soda clear a green pool?

    Not likely! Baking soda raises pH, which can worsen algae growth. Green pool usually indicates algae problems, requiring stronger solutions like shocking or algaecides to eliminate them. Baking soda might be helpful for minor pH adjustments later!

    What can ruin a chlorine pool?

    High chlorine levels, improper pH balance, and neglecting basic maintenance can damage your pool. Always follow chlorine dosage instructions and test water regularly to maintain optimal levels. Skim, brush, and vacuum routinely to prevent debris build-up that can affect chlorine effectiveness!

    Is it OK to use shock instead of chlorine?

    Shock (high dose chlorine) kills existing algae and bacteria, but shouldn't replace regular chlorine usage. Shock is a temporary treatment, while chlorine provides ongoing sanitation. Maintain consistent chlorine levels for continuous protection!

    Do you use algaecide or shock first?

    For a green pool, shock first! Shock treatment effectively kills existing algae, while algaecides mainly prevent future growth. Use algaecide after shocking to maintain control and prevent algae from returning!
