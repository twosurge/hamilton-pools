    How long does it take to clean a really dirty pool?

    Depends on the level of neglect! Expect several hours for extensive cleaning. Removing debris, scrubbing surfaces, vacuuming thoroughly, and adjusting water chemistry might take a whole day. Be patient and tackle it step-by-step!

    Can you get a UTI from a chlorinated pool?

    Technically possible, but unlikely. UTIs usually stem from bacterial imbalances within the body. However, poorly maintained or heavily chlorinated pools can irritate skin and mucous membranes, potentially increasing susceptibility to infections. Maintain proper water balance and hygiene practices to minimize risks!

    How do you clean a neglected pool?

    Tackle it in stages! Start by removing large debris and skimming the surface. Shock the pool to kill algae and bacteria. Brush and vacuum thoroughly to remove settled dirt. Test and adjust water chemistry. Finally, maintain regular cleaning and chemical balance to prevent future neglect!

    Why did my pool turn green after I shocked it?

    Dead algae! Shocking kills algae, but doesn't magically make them disappear. The dead algae cells can cloud the water temporarily before the filter removes them. Vacuuming the pool thoroughly after shocking helps speed up the clearing process!

    How do you get dirt off the bottom of a pool without a vacuum?

    No worries! Grab a pool brush and scrub the floor to loosen debris. Sweep it towards the deep end using a pool broom. Then, use a net or pool skimmer to scoop up the accumulated dirt from the deep end. It's a good workout, but effective!

    Can you over shock a dirty pool?

    Yes, over-shocking can damage your pool and harm swimmers. Follow recommended dosages based on pool size and follow-up instructions. If unsure, consult a professional to avoid damaging your pool or creating unsafe conditions!
