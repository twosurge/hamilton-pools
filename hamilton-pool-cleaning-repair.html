    What are the 4 steps to a clean pool?

    Skim the surface, brush the walls and floor, vacuum the bottom, and test and adjust water chemistry. These four steps, done regularly, will keep your pool sparkling and safe for swimming.

    How do I clean my pool like a professional?

    Master the basics: skimming, brushing, vacuuming, and chemical balancing. Invest in quality tools like a telescopic pole and leaf net. Shock the pool occasionally to combat algae blooms. Test water frequently and adjust chemicals precisely. Crystal-clear pool guaranteed!

    What time of day is best to shock a pool?*

    Evening is ideal! Sunlight burns off chlorine faster, so nighttime shocking optimizes its effectiveness. It also minimizes swimmer exposure during the peak use period. Remember, follow manufacturer instructions for specific shock products and pool types!

    Should you shock or brush a pool first?

    Always brush your pool before shocking it. Brushing loosens debris and algae, allowing the shock treatment to reach and effectively kill contaminants throughout the water. Shocking before brushing would only sanitize the surface layer, leaving hidden impurities.

    What to do with a pool you no longer want?

    Don't abandon it! Safely decommission your pool to avoid hazards and property damage. Consider filling it in, converting it to a water feature, or donating it for community use. Consult local regulations and pool professionals for safe and responsible options!

    Do you really need to shock pool weekly?

    Not necessarily! Weekly shocking is recommended during peak season or heavy usage. Generally, shock every 1-2 weeks unless algae blooms or cloudy water appear, indicating the need for an extra kick!
